Our Story

Built on borrowed recipes, kept on purpose

Every dish on our menu started somewhere else — a railway platform, a college gate, a wedding kitchen. We didn't invent street food; we just refused to let it disappear once it went online.

Our founder spent two years travelling between Maharashtra's stalls and carts, trading recipes for labour, before opening the first Mirchi & Co. counter in 2019. The rule has stayed the same since day one: nothing is cooked until it's ordered.

Today we run one central kitchen and a small fleet of riders, but the food still tastes like it came off a cart — because, in every way that matters, it did.

Milestones

How we got here

2019

One cart, one crossing

The first Mirchi & Co. stall opens near Karjat station, serving vada pav and chaas to commuters.

2021

The recipe book grows

Menu expands to 20+ dishes after a year of trading recipes with vendors across Maharashtra.

2023

First delivery kitchen

We move off the cart and into a proper kitchen, built to fire tandoor orders for delivery.

2026

32 dishes, one ticket at a time

Today's menu, still cooked to order, still delivered by riders who know the neighbourhood.
